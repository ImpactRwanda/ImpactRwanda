The Journey

From student entrepreneur to international mentor, this journey began with a simple question: How can I offer my skills and resources to support those in need?

The Beginning

In 2021, I began creating and selling artwork, developing it into a small business. As a sophomore at Trinity Academy, my graphic design class sparked a deeper passion for how design can communicate ideas and create impact.

Rwanda Discovery

Traveling to Rwanda through Apostles Raleigh opened my eyes to the "land of a thousand hills." Meeting youth and women with great entrepreneurial spirit but limited resources sparked a desire to share my skills and resources.

Partnership with Mother's Union

Meeting Clementine Dushimimana, a key leader in the Mother's Union (est. 1955, 3,050+ women), led to developing a collaborative project focused on digital art and graphic design training for young women.

February 2024 Training

Returned to Rwanda with iPads, design software, printers, and a comprehensive curriculum. Spent intensive weeks training young women in "soft design" - digital graphic design skills that could lead to sustainable income.

Partnership in Action

From Students to Entrepreneurs

The young women I trained in February 2024 have formed their own collaborative partnership, working together to develop their skills and create a sustainable design business. They've evolved from students learning the basics to service providers creating professional marketing materials for churches and businesses throughout their community.

Collaborative Partnership

Rather than working individually, the trainees recognized the power of collaboration. They formed a partnership that allows them to take on larger projects, share skills, and support each other's growth.

Real Client Work

They're now creating professional marketing materials including logos, flyers, banners, and promotional content for local churches and businesses, building both their portfolio and their reputation.

Skill Development

Through ongoing practice and collaboration, their design skills continue to improve. Each project presents new challenges that push their creative and technical abilities forward.

Ongoing Mentorship

Lessons Learned

Key insights from cross-cultural training, mentorship, and partnership building that could benefit others pursuing similar initiatives.

Partnership Over Charity

The most sustainable impact comes from true partnership. Rather than simply providing resources, collaborating as equals creates lasting change and mutual respect.

Local Leadership is Essential

Working with established leaders like Clementine and the Mother's Union provided cultural context, community trust, and ongoing support that external efforts alone couldn't achieve.

Skills + Community = Success

Teaching technical skills worked best when combined with fostering community among trainees. Their collaborative partnership multiplied individual capabilities.

Ongoing Support Matters

Initial training was just the beginning. Continued mentorship, feedback, and business guidance proved crucial for translating skills into sustainable income.

Cultural Adaptation

Design principles are universal, but application must consider local aesthetics, cultural values, and market preferences to be truly effective.

Technology as Tool, Not Solution

iPads and software were enablers, but success came from combining technology with hands-on training, ongoing support, and community building.

Building Toward Independence

Current Stage: Skill Refinement

The partnership is actively taking on client work while continuing to develop their technical skills and build their reputation in the community.

Next Phase: Income Generation

Focus on establishing consistent pricing, expanding client base, and generating reliable income that can support their families and personal goals.

Long-term Vision: Business Expansion

Goal of growing into a recognized design business that can train others, take on larger projects, and become a sustainable source of creative services in Rwanda.

Legacy Goal: Teaching Others

Ultimate vision of the partnership becoming mentors themselves, training the next generation of young women in graphic design and entrepreneurship.
